Home Page for Artificial Intelligence in Films

Starting from the sci-fi movie Metropolis, attempts have been made to depict a world dominated by technology, venturing into a future where intelligent robots roam the earth and underprivileged citizens suffer due to the high cost of living. This course will blend a study of the roadmaps of current tech giants and sci-fi movies to understand the ethical, scientific and political decisions we have to make now for a better and sustainable future.

The focus here is on artificial intelligence itself, not the robotic form, if any, it takes.

Some questions to be discussed during the course:

  • Can a good man create a bad robot? Can a bad man create a good robot? [Ref: criminals have good off springs and criminals have good parents. Is there a correlation?]
  • Why do we desire to create life? God and Golem, Inc?
  • Our sense of “belonging” to someone is linked to our sense of touch. Unseen Gods are supplemented by physical objects. All major religions with unseen gods have physical objects: buildings, coffins, crosses, etc. to go with their love of god. Will Alexa provide the ‘physical’ part of an intelligent entity?
  • Can a trained Alexa be part of a robotic body after many years of training? What type of psychological issue it will create?
  • Imagine a society where a big percentage of people are emotionally affiliated with AI. What will be that society?
  • Amputees have sensation of having their lost hands/limbs still there. What if the robotic arm starts making its own decisions?
  • If a human falls in love with AI, what will she/he expect in return? How will she feel satisfied that she is getting real love in return?
  • Religious views on AI: what do Jewish, Muslim, Christian, Hindu orthodox priests think? How will they handle AI?
  • What is the difference between an emotionally insensitive / damaged human being and a robot?

Ethical Issues
